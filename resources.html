Overview

Who this helps: people living with spinal cord injuries (SCI) in Mobile and the Gulf Coast, plus caregivers and veterans.

What’s here: local transportation and equipment programs, prescription savings, grants for home modifications, rehab/exercise options, and peer support—vetted by our group.

Need help applying? Call the Independent Living Center of Mobile at (251) 460-0301 and ask for SCI peer support.

Last updated: November 7, 2025.

This page compiles national and Alabama-specific programs that help spinal cord injury survivors, veterans, caregivers, and families afford medications, home modifications, durable medical equipment, and everyday support. Always confirm current eligibility requirements directly with each program.

Assistive Technology Loans & DME Reuse

⚡ Need a new Wheelchair? For newly injured spinal cord injury patients, obtaining a brand-new wheelchair can feel overwhelming and frustrating. These steps help you get approved.
  • Face-to-face chart notes
    Ask your doctor to create formal chart notes for the visit, clearly stating why you need power mobility and any special features (tilt/recline).
  • Prescription
    The doctor must prescribe the power wheelchair and order a PT/OT evaluation. Have them fax notes + Rx to the DME provider.
  • PT / OT evaluation
    The provider schedules this after receiving the prescription; measurements finalize the chair setup for insurance.
  • Why it matters
    Notes + prescription form the documentation your provider submits to insurance for authorization.
